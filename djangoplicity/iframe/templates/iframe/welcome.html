Welcome to the official page of the European Southern Observatory!
If you love discovering the Universe like we do, you'll find here the latest news from ESO - the most productive observatory in the world and a platform for mega science projects in astronomy. New findings, images and videos of the Universe, competitions, events and much more will all be presented here, so keep in touch!

Founded in 1962 as an intergovernmental astronomy organisation, ESO has today 15 Member states. Its mission is to encourage and facilitate cooperation in and beyond Europe, allowing people of various cultures to work together to explore the Universe.

ESO develops and operates world-class observing facilities for ground-based astronomical research in the southern hemisphere. Its telescopes are based in Chile, in the Atacama Desert, one of the best places in the world for observations.

At the La Silla Observatory, at an altitude of 2400 metres, ESO operates some of the most productive 4-metre class telescopes in the world.
The Very Large Telescope (VLT) is the world's most advanced optical instrument, consisting of four Unit Telescopes with main mirrors of 8.2m diameter and four movable 1.8m diameter Auxiliary Telescopes.
Together with its international partners, ESO is building ALMA, the most powerful telescope for observing the cool Universe. Acting as a single telescope, ALMA will be composed initially of 66 high-precision antennas.
With its 40-meter class mirror, the European Extremely Large Telescope will be the biggest eye on the sky, tackling the biggest scientific challenges of our time, including tracking down Earth-like planets around other stars where life could exist.
